The East Coast is home to the oldest continuously operating lighthouse in the U.S., Sandy Hook Lighthouse in New Jersey, built in 1764.

The West Coast’s redwood forests in California include Hyperion, the world’s tallest living tree, standing over 380 feet.

The South’s Atchafalaya Basin in Louisiana is the largest swamp in North America, spanning nearly a million acres.
